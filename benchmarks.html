The AuspexAI Drift Benchmark

“Drift” is how much a model's answers change, measured against how much it naturally varies run to run. 1× is that normal variation: anything below it is no real change; a value of 4 means the answers moved four times further than two identical runs ever do on their own. Every measurement compares a model's answers on the same fixed prompts against one calibrated baseline run.

How to read it

Each plot is one model at one temperature — blue at temperature 0, orange above it. Rightward = the single biggest change in any one answer is larger; upward = more of the answers changed at all. The shaded strip on the left is no real change. So the bottom-left is steady, and the top-right drifts far and across the board. Hover a model to connect its two temperatures:

  • Two plots on the same spot: the model drifts just as hard at temperature 0 as above it — the instability is baked in, the worst case for reproducibility.
  • Two plots far apart: steady at temperature 0 down in the bottom-left, and only temperature above 0 pushes it out — the drift is a knob you control.

Why you can trust it

Every number is published by the researcher who ran the experiment, cryptographically signed, and anchored in a public transparency log — this page re-checks each signature in your browser, and the links in the table go to the public log records. Nothing here is hand-entered or editable: results are admitted automatically, by verification, not by an editor. Technical readers can re-derive every number from the underlying signed evidence — the raw registry and the open AuspexAI tooling make each figure independently reproducible.
