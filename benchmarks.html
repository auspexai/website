How to read this

Each row is one model; each dot is one measurement of that model's behavior on the same fixed set of prompts, compared against a single calibrated baseline run. The further right the dot, the further the responses moved from the baseline — 1 on this scale is the noise floor, the amount identical setups naturally wobble, so anything left of that line is "no real change" and everything past it is genuine behavioral difference, measured in multiples of that natural wobble. The small text under each dot says the conditions (deterministic, with randomness, or the same setup simply re-measured later) and how many of the prompt sets moved. Comparing dots across rows compares models; comparing dots within a row shows how conditions change one model's behavior.

Why you can trust it

Every number is published by the researcher who ran the experiment, cryptographically signed, and anchored in a public transparency log — this page re-checks each signature in your browser, and the links under every bar go to the public log records. Nothing here is hand-entered or editable: results are admitted to this page automatically, by verification, not by an editor. Technical readers can re-derive every number from the underlying signed evidence — the raw registry and the open AuspexAI tooling make each figure independently reproducible.
