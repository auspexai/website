The AuspexAI Drift Benchmark

Our apparatus, measured. Each row scores one experiment's behavior against a fixed, signed baseline — the same model-serving stack, probed repeatedly over time and across configurations. Every number on this page traces to cryptographically signed, publicly anchored evidence. Don't trust the chart — verify the cell.

How to read it. Drift is measured in envelope units (EU): each declared behavioral feature's shift, divided by that feature's calibrated tolerance — the empirically measured noise floor of the same configuration on a heterogeneous volunteer fleet. 1.0 EU is the boundary between noise and drift; the magnitude reads as "how many times the noise floor did behavior move." The headline is a pair: peak (the worst probe) and breadth (the fraction of probes beyond the envelope). Byte-level divergence and within-run disagreement are reported separately — never folded into the score. Scores are comparable only within a track (one baseline = one envelope); comparing across tracks is a stated non-goal.

Verify any entry yourself. Each entry is an Ed25519-signed claim by the publishing researcher (this page also checks the signatures in your browser). The attestation links go to the public Rekor transparency log; deep verification re-derives every number from the signed evidence bundles: 
pip install auspexai-tenant
auspexai-tenant benchmark verify-entry entry.json     # the signed claim
auspexai-tenant bundle verify bundle.json             # the evidence itself
auspexai-tenant benchmark drift OBS.json REF.json     # recompute the score
Methodology: the Drift Benchmark standard (envelope units, peak + breadth, calibrated tolerances) is part of the open AuspexAI platform. Registry: benchmarks.json (curated; entries are tenant-signed and inclusion is deliberate).
